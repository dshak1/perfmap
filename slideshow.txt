Why it slaps: Abseil is what Google uses instead of raw STL

How to Pass the Technical Interview with Mehrdad Momenizadeh 
The word Leetcode strikes fear into the hearts of CS students. But why?
Even with tools like cluely and claude 4.6 being better than pretty much any single engineer, leetcode is still around. Companies like Meta, have leaned into this and have allowed and encouraged the use of AI for their technical interviews because even with them its still not enough. Why because they dont only want to see you get the right answer but they want to see you reason, they want to hear your thoughts, they want to see you get stuck and what you do about it. We’re gonna cover this in depth, here to help me with this is my brother Mehrdad, a published machine learning researcher in Delta lab and an incoming ML engineer at Huawei.
*let mehrdad do his thing
The difficulty, time pressure, stage fright. Preparing is hard, it requires you to know and be able to apply stuff we learned in CMPT 225 and CMPT 307. 
Not only that, getting a working solution is sometimes not enough, we need to consider runtime, and space complexity. 
And if you can’t articulate your reasoning and intuition, its hard not to assume you were using AI
*Hear a Googler speak about his approach and demonstrate his methodical process irl (Mehrdad, Sri)
Personally, seeing people I put on pedestals walk through it and very humanly, at first, be lost but then methodically break it down and arrive at a solution helped break down the barrier to start more practice. You do a few and they start to become repetitive and doable. At first you had no clue then watch neetcode, then you had an idea (which was wrong) then watched neetcode, then I had an idea (where part of it was correct) then I watched neetcode, etc. Thing is, you gotta keep going.
